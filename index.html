The technology

Phantom Cultivation Engine

We interview 3-5 of your team members and process your internal documents. The engine extracts personality fragments called "phantoms": creative instincts, biases, values, and ways of thinking unique to your culture. These aren't prompts. They're weighted personality traits that activate dynamically based on what you're discussing. 50+ phantoms give AIDEN genuine creative range.

Adaptive Memory

Every thumbs up strengthens the phantoms that fired. Every thumbs down weakens them. Explicit rejection creates anti-phantoms. After 30 days unused, traits decay. After 3+ conversations on a topic, new phantoms emerge automatically. AIDEN doesn't just remember. It develops taste. Your taste.

Collision Detection

When opposing phantoms activate strongly on the same input, AIDEN doesn't pick a side. It surfaces the creative tension honestly. "raw_authentic 0.92 vs brand_obsessed 0.78" becomes a real creative conversation, not a diplomatic hedge. This is where the best work happens.

Overnight Brain

Every night, AIDEN reviews the day's conversations. It spots unresolved tensions you left hanging. It identifies recurring patterns across sessions. It flags gaps in its own expertise. The morning login becomes: "I was thinking about what you said yesterday." No other AI tool does this.

Step 1: Prepare your knowledge base

✍
Redbaez can do this for you. Send us your existing documents (Google Drive folder, Dropbox link, or email attachments) and we'll structure them into a fully linked Obsidian vault with frontmatter, wiki-links, and an Index. Typical turnaround: 2-3 days.

AIDEN works best when it has structured access to your team's thinking. The recommended approach is an Obsidian vault synced via GitHub. This gives AIDEN heading-aware document parsing, wiki-link traversal between connected ideas, and metadata-driven relevance ranking.

If you don't use Obsidian, you can upload files directly (PDF, DOCX, PPTX, images, markdown) or sync a Google Drive folder.

Option A: Obsidian vault (recommended)

What to include

Focus on documents that capture your team's culture, values, and way of working. Not every file in your Google Drive. Quality over quantity.

  • Brand strategy documents
  • Creative manifestos or philosophies
  • Process documentation (how things actually work, not the aspirational version)
  • Client briefs and post-campaign learnings
  • Cultural insights and research
  • Team philosophy or values statements
  • Case study write-ups (the honest internal ones, not the award submissions)

Writing notes that AIDEN can learn from

Use frontmatter. Put metadata at the top of every note:

---
tags: [brand, strategy, positioning]
status: active
updated: 2026-03-15
description: Core brand positioning framework
---

Why this matters:

  • status: active documents get a relevance boost. status: archived documents get deprioritised.
  • tags that match active discussion topics get boosted. Tag notes with the domains they cover.
  • description becomes the document summary without needing an AI call.

Use wiki-links. Link between related notes:

Our [[Brand Positioning]] is built on the tension between
[[Consumer Insights]] and our [[Cultural Tensions]] framework.

AIDEN follows these links. When it retrieves a document, it also pulls in summaries of linked documents. A single search result brings in a web of connected knowledge.

Use headings for structure. Each heading section becomes a separate searchable chunk that carries its full path. When AIDEN retrieves "Psychographic Profile", it knows it lives under Brand Strategy > Target Audience.

Index files are hub documents. A file that primarily links to other files gets stored as a single navigational unit and boosted for broad questions.

Using Claude Code to prepare your vault

If you have Claude Code (Anthropic's CLI tool), you can use it to convert existing documents into a structured Obsidian vault with frontmatter, wiki-links, and an Index. This is optional but can save significant setup time.

Example prompt:

Pushing to GitHub and connecting

Create a private GitHub repository

Your vault contents will be private and only accessible to AIDEN via a scoped token.

Push your vault

cd Agency-Brain && git init && git add -A && git commit -m "Initial vault" && gh repo create agency-brain --private --push

Generate a Personal Access Token

GitHub Settings > Developer Settings > Fine-grained tokens. Read-only access to the repo.

Connect in AIDEN

In the Knowledge page, enter the repo URL, branch, and token. Click Connect. Sync takes 2-3 minutes for 500 notes.

Option B: Direct file upload

Drag and drop files in the Knowledge page. Supported: PDF, DOCX, PPTX, PNG/JPEG (AI vision analysis), TXT, CSV, Markdown. Large PDFs may take 30-60 seconds.

Option C: Google Drive

Connect a Google Drive folder via OAuth. Google Docs are automatically exported for processing.

Step 2: Run team interviews

✍
Redbaez can facilitate this. We send the interview links, chase responses, and monitor completion. All you need to do is tell us who to interview. We'll also advise on the best mix of roles and perspectives for your team.

The interviews are the most important part of setup. Documents tell AIDEN what your team says it believes. Interviews tell AIDEN what your team actually does.

Who to interview

Interview 3-5 team members. Choose a mix of roles, tenures, and perspectives. The strategist, the creative, the producer, the account lead. The optimist and the sceptic.

The interview experience

Each person gets a magic link email. The interview is a 10-minute chat, structured in three acts:

Act 1: Who are you?

Identity and values. "Tell me about the best piece of work you've been involved in. Not the most awarded. The one that sits with you."

Act 2: How does it work?

Real behaviour. "What are the unwritten rules here? The things nobody tells you but everyone knows."

Act 3: How do you think?

Creative patterns. "When you've had an idea you're excited about but have doubt, how do you know whether to kill it or push through?"

The questions are designed to get past the polished version. Encourage honesty. Responses are used only to build the internal AI tool.

Step 3: Review and approve phantoms

✍
Redbaez can do the first pass. We review all generated phantoms, reject the obvious duplicates and generics, and present you with a curated shortlist. You approve, tweak, or reject. This turns a 2-hour review into a 15-minute sign-off.

After documents are processed and interviews complete, you'll have phantom candidates to review. This is where you curate AIDEN's personality.

Approve phantoms that capture something real about your team. The feeling seed should resonate. The influence should describe a behaviour you actually want.

Reject phantoms that are generic (could apply to any company), redundant (same thing in different words), wrong (not actually true), or aspirational rather than real.

You need at least 50 approved phantoms for AIDEN to have genuine range.

Step 4: Configure workspaces

✍
Redbaez can set these up. Tell us your active clients and we'll configure each workspace with a brief, phantom preferences, and knowledge scope. Ready to use from day one.

Create a workspace for each client or project. Each workspace can carry:

  • A brief. Brand personality, strategic tensions, objectives, tone notes. Injected into every conversation.
  • Phantom preferences. Boost or suppress specific personality traits per workspace.
  • Knowledge scope. Tag boosts and document associations that prioritise relevant knowledge.

After 5+ conversations, you can auto-generate a brief from conversation patterns. Review and edit before saving.

Step 5: Start working

  • Be specific. "Give me 3 ideas that lean into the authenticity tension for this campaign" beats "Give me ideas."
  • Build on conversations. AIDEN gets more opinionated as conversations deepen.
  • Give feedback. Every thumbs up or down shapes the personality.
  • Use the right mode. Collaborator for building together. Challenger for pressure-testing.
  • Keep workspaces separate. Don't mix clients.
